**Good news: You drive on the RIGHT side** Iceland drives on the right, just like California. Steering wheel is on the left. You'll feel at home immediately.

**⚠️ Bay Area habit to break: 90 km/h feels painfully slow** You're used to cruising at 65-75 mph on 101/280. Iceland's max highway speed is 56 mph equivalent. Don't fight it — the scenery is the point, and speed cameras are everywhere. Fines start at 30,000 ISK (~$220) and the rental company will charge your card.

### Roundabouts (you'll see dozens)

**Key difference from the US:** The inner lane has right of way over the outer lane when exiting. This is the opposite of what you might expect.

-   **Entering:** Yield to traffic already in the roundabout (same as CA)
-   **Two-lane roundabout:** Right lane = first exit. Left lane = second exit or beyond
-   **Exiting from inner lane:** You have priority — outer lane vehicles must yield to you
-   **Signal:** Use right turn signal when exiting

### Headlights

**💡 Headlights must be ON at all times** Day or night, summer or winter, rain or shine. Your rental car probably has automatic headlights, but double-check. In June you'll have ~21 hours of daylight — headlights are still mandatory. This is enforced.

### Other Rules

-   **No right turn on red** — ever. Wait for the green.
-   **Seatbelts mandatory** for ALL passengers, including rear seats
-   **Blood alcohol limit:** 0.05% (stricter than CA's 0.08%) — practically zero tolerance
-   **Mobile phone:** Hands-free only. Use a mount or don't touch it.
-   **Off-road driving is ILLEGAL** — you will be fined heavily. Iceland's ecosystem is fragile; tire tracks last decades in moss.

## ⚠️Iceland-Specific Hazards

Things you'll never encounter on 101 or Page Mill Road.

🐑

#### Sheep on Roads

Roaming sheep are everywhere June-September. They cross without warning, often in pairs — if you see one, the lamb is right behind it. Slow down to 30 km/h near sheep. If you hit one, you must notify the farmer and may owe compensation (~$300-500).

🌉

#### Single-Lane Bridges

Marked with "Einbreið brú" signs. The car CLOSER to the bridge has right of way. Flash your lights to signal you're going. Don't try to squeeze past — pull into the widened shoulder and wait.

🪨

#### Gravel-to-Paved Transitions

The #1 cause of tourist accidents. When paved road suddenly becomes gravel (or vice versa), your traction changes instantly. SLOW DOWN before the transition. Don't brake hard on gravel.

💨

#### Extreme Wind

Wind gusts of 50+ mph are common, especially near mountain passes and in the east. Your Renault Trafic van is a tall target. Use both hands. Car doors can be ripped off hinges — always hold your door when opening. Check vedur.is before driving.

🫣

#### Blind Hills & Curves

Iceland's roads follow the terrain. "Blind hill" signs (blindhæð) mean you can't see oncoming traffic. Stay in your lane, slow down, and never pass on a blind hill. These are nothing like Bay Area's gentle grades.

🌊

#### River Crossings (F-roads)

Your rental van is NOT allowed on F-roads (highland roads with river fords). Don't attempt these — they void your insurance completely. Stick to Route 1 and numbered roads.

**🚫 The #1 tourist driving mistake in Iceland** Stopping on the road to take photos. There is no shoulder on most Icelandic roads — just a gravel drop-off. If you stop, you WILL cause a rear-end collision. Always pull completely off the road into a designated pullout. They're marked and frequent along Route 1.

## 🛣️Route 1 (Ring Road)

The 1,322 km highway that circles Iceland. Your likely route for Jun 18-29.

#### Route 1 Quick Facts

-   **Total length:** 1,322 km (821 miles) — about the distance from SF to Portland
-   **Driving time (non-stop):** ~16 hours — but nobody does it non-stop
-   **Recommended days:** 7-10 days minimum for the full ring
-   **Surface:** ~98% paved as of 2026, a few gravel sections remain in the east
-   **Lanes:** Mostly 2 lanes (one each direction), no median
-   **Gas stations:** Every 50-100 km on average, but 200+ km gaps exist in the east and north

### Fuel Strategy

**⛽ Never pass a gas station below half tank** Unlike the Bay Area where there's a Chevron every 2 miles, Icelandic stations can be 200 km apart in the east. Your Renault Trafic diesel van has roughly a 60-80L tank (~450-550 km range). Fill up at every opportunity outside the Reykjavik area. Most stations accept credit cards at unmanned pumps — you'll need a card with a PIN (not a signature card).

## 🚐Your Rental: Renault Trafic

Specific tips for your 9-seater diesel van.

⛽

#### Diesel Only

Your van takes DIESEL (dísel), not petrol/benzín. The nozzle is usually black (not green). Misfuelling will destroy the engine and void insurance. Double-check every time.

📏

#### It's Big

The Trafic is 5m long and 1.97m tall. Watch your height in parking garages (most have a 2.0m limit). Practice reversing before leaving KEF. Use your mirrors — Icelandic roads are narrow.

💨

#### Wind Target

A tall van catches crosswinds hard. Grip the wheel with both hands on exposed coastal and highland sections. If gusts exceed 25 m/s (56 mph), consider stopping. Check vedur.is.

🚫

#### No F-Roads

Your 2WD van is NOT allowed on F-roads (mountain/highland roads). These require 4WD + high clearance. Attempting F-roads voids ALL insurance. Stick to paved and numbered gravel roads.

### Insurance You Should Know About

-   **CDW (Collision Damage Waiver):** Likely included — covers body damage with a deductible
-   **SCDW (Super CDW):** Reduces the deductible to zero or near-zero — worth it for a van
-   **SAAP/Gravel Protection:** Covers gravel/rock chip damage — **highly recommended** since Route 1 has gravel sections and you WILL encounter flying rocks from other vehicles
-   **Sand & Ash Protection:** Covers volcanic ash/sand damage — relevant for south coast
-   **Tire & Windshield:** Usually not covered by standard CDW — ask at pickup

**💳 Your Capital One Venture X has rental car coverage** But verify it covers Iceland and 9-seater vans specifically. Some cards exclude vehicles over 7 passengers or rentals longer than 30 days. Call the number on the back before your trip.

### What To Do If...

-   **You hit a sheep:** Call the rental company AND the local police (112). You must try to locate the farmer. You may owe ~$300-500 compensation.
-   **Flat tire:** Pull completely off the road. Your van has a spare under the cargo area. Call the rental company if unsure — they'll send someone.
-   **Car won't start:** Call the rental company's 24/7 line. Keep warm — carry blankets/layers even in summer.
-   **Lost/no signal:** Drive to the nearest farm — Icelanders are incredibly helpful and almost everyone speaks English.

## 🧠Quick Quiz

Test yourself before you get behind the wheel in Iceland.

1\. What side of the road does Iceland drive on?

Correct! Iceland drives on the right, same as California. You'll feel right at home.

2\. The maximum speed on a paved highway in Iceland is:

90 km/h (56 mph) is the max on paved highways. That's about 10 mph slower than Highway 101. Speed cameras are everywhere.

3\. When must your headlights be on in Iceland?

Headlights must be on at all times in Iceland — even in June when you'll have 21 hours of daylight. It's the law.

4\. You approach a single-lane bridge. Who has right of way?

The car closer to the bridge has right of way. The other car should pull into the widened area and wait. Flash your lights to signal you're crossing.

5\. Your Renault Trafic takes:

Your van is diesel! Misfuelling with petrol will destroy the engine and is NOT covered by insurance. The diesel nozzle is usually black.

6\. Can you turn right on a red light in Iceland?

No right on red in Iceland, ever. This is a common habit for Bay Area drivers to break. Wait for the green.

7\. You see a sheep on the road. What do you do?

Slow way down and look for a second sheep — they travel in pairs (mother + lamb). The lamb often darts across the road after the mother. Never honk or swerve suddenly. If you hit a sheep, you must notify the farmer.
